Speak with a Fine Accent: Text-to-Speech Synthesis with Controllable Accent and its Intensity

anonymous

Abstract
Accented text-to-speech (TTS) synthesis seeks to generate speech in the primary language (L1) with an accent in the second language (L2). Accented TTS synthesis is challenging as L2-accented speech may vary in speaking style more than the L1 speech. Furthermore, the control of accent intensity in an utterance is the key to accent rendering. In this work, we propose a neural TTS solution with controllable accent and its intensity, that is referred to as CAI-TTS system. The CAI-TTS system employs three novel mechanisms: 1) a novel accent variance adaptor to model the complex accent variance with three controlling factors, that are related to prosody, namely pitch, energy and duration; 2) an elegant accent intensity learning strategy to quantify the accent intensity; 3) a novel consistency constraint module to encourage the TTS system to render the expected accent intensity at a fine level. Experiments show that the CAI-TTS system attains superior performance to the baseline models in terms of accent rendering and intensity control. To our best knowledge, this is the first study of accented TTS synthesis with explicit intensity control.
